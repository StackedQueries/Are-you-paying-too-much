Short-Term vs ACA Health Insurance

Understanding the tradeoffs between short-term and marketplace coverage.

Short-term health plans are often cheaper than ACA (marketplace) plans — but they are underwritten, can exclude pre-existing conditions, and may not cover certain care (e.g. back, neck, spine).

ACA plans are guaranteed-issue and cover essential health benefits, but premiums and deductibles can be high — especially if you don’t qualify for subsidies.

For self-employed and contractors, the right choice depends on your health history, risk tolerance, and budget. A short-term plan might save money month-to-month, but one major claim could leave you exposed.
