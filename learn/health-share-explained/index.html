Health Share Plans Explained

They’re cheaper than insurance — but they’re not insurance. Here’s what you need to know.

Health share plans (sometimes called health share ministries or Christian co-ops) are not health insurance. They are voluntary cost-sharing arrangements where members contribute monthly and the organization helps pay medical bills from a shared pool.

Key differences from insurance: there is no insurance contract, no guarantee of payment, and no regulation by state insurance departments. Claims are paid at the discretion of the organization or directly by other members.

Health shares are often marketed as affordable alternatives to ACA plans. Monthly costs can be significantly lower. But the tradeoff is real: if you have a major claim, there is no legal obligation for the plan to pay.
